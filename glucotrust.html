Gymnema Sylvestre

Gymnema Sylvestre is a perennial woody vine native to Asia, Australia, and Africa that is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine. It is believed to help reduce sugar absorption in the digestive system and support healthy blood sugar levels.

Juniper Berries

Juniper Berries are derived from the juniper tree and are known for their potent antioxidant properties. They have been used historically to treat a variety of ailments and are thought to help with inflammation and blood sugar control.
3

Cinnamon

Cinnamon is a spice obtained from the inner bark of several tree species from the genus Cinnamomum. It is used for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory properties and the ability to influence blood sugar levels.
4

Chromium

Chromium is an essential trace mineral that is important for the metabolism of nutrients and the regulation of insulin, playing a significant role in blood sugar management.
5

Zinc

Zinc is a vital mineral crucial for immune function, protein synthesis, and cell division. It is also involved in insulin production and may help protect against diabetes-related complications.
6

Biotin

Biotin, also known as vitamin B7, is involved in a wide range of metabolic processes, primarily related to the utilization of fats, carbohydrates, and amino acids. It is often recommended for supporting metabolic health.
7

Licorice Root

Licorice Root has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. It contains compounds that may support digestive health and has been studied for its potential to affect blood sugar and support metabolic function.
8

Manganese

Manganese is a trace mineral that plays a role in bone formation, blood clotting, and reducing inflammation. It is also involved in the metabolism of carbohydrates and lipids, which may influence blood sugar levels.
9

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is an essential nutrient that acts as an antioxidant. It is important for the maintenance of skin, blood vessels, bones, and cartilage, and it also may have an effect on blood sugar regulation.
10

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is a fat-soluble antioxidant that protects cell membranes from oxidative damage. It's involved in immune function and, among other roles, may also play a part in regulating blood sugar levels.
